Property Conveyancing Tips in Australia

Your guide to a smooth property buying or selling experience

What is Conveyancing?

Conveyancing is the legal process of transferring property ownership from one person to another. It involves contract review, searches, settlements, and ensuring all legal requirements are met. In Australia, it's essential for both residential and commercial properties to avoid costly mistakes.

Key Tips for Buyers

  • Get pre-approval for finance before making offers.
  • Review the Contract of Sale and Section 32 (Vendor Statement) carefully.
  • Conduct thorough property searches (e.g., title, council, flood risks).
  • Engage a professional early to negotiate terms.
  • Plan for settlement adjustments like rates and taxes.

Key Tips for Sellers

  • Prepare a accurate Vendor Statement to avoid disputes.
  • Disclose any known issues upfront.
  • Choose a fixed-fee conveyancer for transparency.
  • Time your sale to align with market conditions.
  • Consider off-the-plan or subdivision implications if applicable.

Common Pitfalls to Avoid

Delays in settlements, missing deadlines, or overlooking stamp duty concessions can lead to financial losses. Always use technology-enabled services for real-time tracking of progress.
